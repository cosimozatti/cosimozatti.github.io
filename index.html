About

I am an economist and public-policy researcher pursuing a dual Master's degree at the London School of Economics and Sciences Po, after graduating 110/110 cum laude from the University of Bologna in Economics, Politics and Social Sciences.

My work sits at the intersection of economics, political economy and political science, since I am convinced that policy questions cannot be answered by microeconomics or by politics alone. I am drawn to the political economy of good jobs, industrial strategy and state capacity, and increasingly to how public and multilateral finance turns into development outcomes on the ground.

In practice this means combining causal inference and stated-preference methods with institutional and qualitative analysis, whether I am screening loan portfolios against climate criteria, designing a discrete choice experiment, or cleaning a 19,000-respondent survey.
